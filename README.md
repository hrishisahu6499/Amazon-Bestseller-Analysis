# Amazon Bestselling Books Analysis

A comprehensive data analysis project examining Amazon's bestselling books to uncover key trends across genres, author performance, user ratings, and pricing dynamics. The project concludes with data-driven strategic recommendations for content positioning and marketing.

## 📌 Project Overview
Understanding market trends in the publishing industry helps authors, publishers, and retailers optimize their inventory, pricing strategies, and marketing campaigns. This project analyzes a dataset of Amazon's bestselling books to discover what drives a book to the top charts and how fiction compares to non-fiction over time.

## 📊 Dataset Features
The analysis evaluates several key attributes of the bestselling books:
* **Name:** Title of the book.
* **Author:** The writer of the book.
* **User Rating:** The average rating given by buyers (out of 5).
* **Reviews:** The total number of consumer reviews.
* **Price:** The retail price of the book.
* **Year:** The year it appeared on the bestseller list.
* **Genre:** The category of the book (Fiction / Non-Fiction).

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools Used
* **Language:** Python
* **Libraries:** * `pandas` & `numpy` for data cleaning, manipulation, and processing.
    * `matplotlib` & `seaborn` for exploratory data visualization.
* **Environment:** Jupyter Notebook

## 🔍 Key Analysis Steps
1.  **Data Cleaning & Exploration:** Checked for missing values, handled duplicate entries, and verified data types across features.
2.  **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):**
    * Analyzed the distribution of book prices and user ratings.
    * Compared market share and average pricing between Fiction and Non-Fiction genres.
    * Identified top-performing authors based on review counts and average ratings.
    * Tracked year-over-year trends in consumer engagement.
3.  **Insights Extraction:** Evaluated correlation patterns between book prices, popularity (reviews), and customer satisfaction (ratings).

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations Included
Based on the analysis, the project outlines actionable strategies for lower-review, high-potential titles (such as specific pop-culture or niche history books):
* **Competitor Keyword Mapping:** Repositioning lower-review books using high-review competitor keywords to capture "overflow" search traffic.
* **Targeting Niche Communities:** Moving away from mass marketing to target specific blogs, forums, and podcasts catering to specialized interests (e.g., American History, Soul Healing).
* **User-Generated Content (UGC) Strategy:** Leveraging social media interactions to generate "soft reviews" and drive outside traffic to retail product pages.
